Status Report

Our original plan was to simulate a fluid by drawing many particles and using a shader to blend water on top of all these particles. However, we realized that using many small particles is a rather poor approximation to a fluid, as it can never show vortices (local angular momentum) and surface tension. So weâ€™ve decided to first work on more accurate fluid simulations using Navier-Stokes, and add a shader to visualize our vector field. To that end, we have preliminary results of a vector field. Currently, we only show the advection effects of a force applied by the mouse. A small demonstration can be seen below:

Video Link

The demo works by using shaders to color pixels on the screen based on the values in our vector grid. The current plan for now is to finish up the simulation, by adding pressure and fixing checkerboarding. If we have time, we may transition to a particle-in-cell simulation, which is closer to what we had originally intended. Further complexity can be added by working on rigid particle-in-cell simulations, or affine particle-in-cell simulations. So our updated schedule looks like: Apr 26-30, finish simulation. May 1-2, create a demo website and make the presentation.
